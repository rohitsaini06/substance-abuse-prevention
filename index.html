Why Prevention Matters

Early Intervention

Starting education early reduces the chances of substance misuse in adolescence and adulthood.

Community Support

Strong community programs provide resources and safe environments for youth and families.

Policy & Education

Advocating for health policies and educational curricula helps drive long-term change.

Common Risk Factors

Peer Pressure

Social influence is a leading cause of initial substance use among teens and young adults.

Mental Health

Untreated depression, anxiety, and trauma often correlate with higher substance use rates.

Family History

Genetics and household environment play a role in the likelihood of substance dependency.
